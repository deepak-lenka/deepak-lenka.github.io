  • Six Easy Pieces - Richard Feynman
    • Richard Feynman is a collection of the most accessible lectures from his famous series, "The Feynman Lectures on Physics." The book introduces readers to the fundamentals of physics, covering topics such as atoms, basic physics, energy, gravitation, quantum mechanics, and the relationship of physics to other fields. Feynman's unique teaching style, marked by his inimitable wit and ability to simplify complex theories, makes these topics accessible to a general audience. The book is devoid of technical jargon and equations, instead filled with wonderful examples and clever illustrations. Feynman's goal is to ignite curiosity and encourage readers to delve deeper into the world of physics. His approach to teaching, which combines practicality, showmanship, and a deep theoretical understanding of nature, makes "Six Easy Pieces" an engaging and enlightening read for anyone interested in understanding the fundamental principles of physics.
  • The 48 Laws Of Power - Robert Green
    • Robert Greene is a comprehensive guide to understanding and navigating power dynamics. The book presents 48 laws based on historical examples spanning 3,000 years, illustrating how power can be gained, wielded, or lost. Greene posits that everyone is part of a never-ending game of power, either as a player or a pawn. The laws cover a wide range of strategies, from never outshining the master to always saying less than necessary. Greene emphasizes the importance of subtlety, cunning, and appearing fair while being ruthless behind the scenes. The book serves as a manual for those seeking to understand power dynamics and improve their ability to handle power effectively. It suggests that mastering these laws can make one a better friend, lover, and person, as it teaches how to make others feel good about themselves, making them dependent on you as a source of pleasure.

Mastery is presented as attainable for anyone willing to dedicate themselves to the rigorous process of continuous learning and practice.
